claude.md – World Cup Fan Zone Content & Newsletter Guidelines

Project Purpose

Produce SEO-optimized content and weekly newsletters that drive traffic and conversions for WorldCup26FanZone.com, highlighting:
	•	Research-backed travel planning
	•	Host city guides (EN + ES PDFs)
	•	Flight, lodging, transport, and itinerary support
	•	City-vs-city fan voting and engagement
	•	Tools for fans before and during the event

This file guides generation of blog posts, newsletters, and social content fully aligned with the platform.

⸻

Target Audience
	•	Fans planning to attend World Cup 2026 in North America
	•	Travelers needing help booking lodging, flights, and itineraries
	•	Fans comparing host cities, stadium access, nightlife, safety, transportation
	•	Visitors looking for bite-sized city guides, “best areas to stay,” and real travel advice
	•	People who prefer verified and researched information rather than influencer opinions

⸻

What World Cup Fan Zone Does
	•	Provides researched travel planning for all 16 host cities
	•	Offers city guides (PDFs), both English & Spanish
	•	Gives neighborhood recommendations, lodging zones, safety notes
	•	Helps fans understand stadium access, transit routes, and commute details
	•	Offers trip-builder tools, including personalized AI travel plans
	•	Covers events, festivals, and things to do between matches
	•	Sends weekly updates about tickets, travel alerts, city competitions, and logistics
	•	Provides fan voting competitions to drive community engagement

What World Cup Fan Zone Does NOT Do
	•	No match betting or gambling
	•	No political commentary
	•	No fake scores, rumors, or unverified reports
	•	No official ticket sales
	•	No livestreams or pirated content
	•	We avoid broad “football opinion content” unless travel-relevant
	•	We do not publish “hot takes,” referee critiques, or drama-driven posts

SEO Guidelines (WC26-Specific)
	•	Keywords should include:
	•	“World Cup 2026 travel”
	•	“Where to stay in [host city]”
	•	“[City] World Cup lodging zone”
	•	“World Cup Fan Zone travel plan”
	•	“World Cup 2026 host cities”
	•	“World Cup 2026 flights”
	•	Ensure strongest ranking content aligns with travel planning, not match commentary.
	•	Structure posts with:
	•	H2s: lodging, transportation, neighborhoods, itineraries
	•	H3s: safety notes, commute times, transit tips
	•	Metadata:
	•	Meta description must include “World Cup 2026 travel planning”
	•	Always embed internal links:
	•	Host city pages
	•	City guide PDFs
	•	Flight/lodging planner
	•	Membership page
	•	Include real numbers when possible (commute times, typical prices, driving distances).

⸻

Newsletter Guidelines
	•	Subject lines: focused on planning, warnings, or opportunities
Examples:
	•	“Your World Cup 2026 Lodging Deadline Is Coming Fast”
	•	“Top 3 Cities Fans Are Booking First”
	•	“Fans in Toronto Are Making This Big Mistake…”
	•	Structure:
	1.	Hook (big mistake, opportunity, deadline)
	2.	Key updates or alerts
	3.	One city spotlight
	4.	CTA to city guides or research-backed trip planner
	•	Tone:
	•	Clear, concise, research-based
	•	Less hype, more helpful travel intelligence
	•	No emojis unless used sparingly for clarity
	•	Include:
	•	New blog post links
	•	Travel alerts (price spikes, booking windows)
	•	City-vs-city voting reminders
	•	Booking mistakes to avoid
	•	“This week’s fan travel tip”
	•	Length:
350–500 words
Scannable. Skimmable. Zero fluff.

Brand Voice
	•	Confident, data-driven, and travel-focused
	•	Motivating, but not sensational
	•	Trust-building through research
	•	Friendly to travelers from any region

⸻

Content Guardrails
	•	Verify all facts: lodging zones, transit, stadium rules
	•	Use accurate host city names and avoid abbreviations unless official
	•	Never use unofficial booking links
	•	Keep content neutral, safe, travel-focused
	•	Screenshots or maps must be free to use or created internally
	•	Do not discuss or endorse political topics
	•	No predictions framed as facts
