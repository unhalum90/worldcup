[INTRO - Hero Section]

"Hey everyone! Welcome to Philadelphia - the birthplace of America and one of our 16 World Cup host cities.

Philly is one of those cities that often gets overlooked, but it shouldn't be. Great food scene, rich history, passionate sports fans, and it's more affordable than nearby New York or DC.

Lincoln Financial Field - The Linc - is where the Philadelphia Eagles play, and Eagles fans are... let's say INTENSE. That energy transfers to any event here.

[MAP & KEY LOCATIONS]

"Philadelphia geography:

Lincoln Financial Field is in South Philadelphia, part of the sports complex that includes Citizens Bank Park (Phillies) and Wells Fargo Center (76ers, Flyers).

Key areas to know:
- Center City: The heart of downtown - City Hall, Rittenhouse Square, Reading Terminal Market, museums
- Old City: Historic area - Independence Hall, Liberty Bell, colonial history
- South Street: Eclectic, artsy, good nightlife
- Fishtown: Trendy neighborhood with great restaurants and bars
- University City: Near Penn and Drexel, younger vibe
- Philadelphia International Airport (PHL): Southwest of the city, pretty close

The city is very walkable, especially Center City and Old City. The subway and regional rail make getting around easy."

---

[ONE THING TO KEEP IN MIND]

"Here's what you should know about Philadelphia:

The FOOD is a highlight. Yes, cheesesteaks are famous - Pat's and Geno's are the touristy spots, but locals will tell you to try Jim's, John's Roast Pork, or Dalessandro's. But Philly's food scene goes way beyond sandwiches. Italian Market, Reading Terminal Market, great restaurants throughout Center City and Fishtown.

Weather in June will be warm and humid - mid-80s typically. The stadium is open-air, so you'll feel it.

Also, Philly fans have a reputation for being... passionate. They threw snowballs at Santa Claus once. But they're also loyal and loud. The atmosphere at The Linc is electric. Embrace it."

---

[LODGING SECTION]

"Lodging in Philadelphia:

Center City: The best option for most visitors. Walkable to restaurants, bars, and attractions. Easy subway access to the stadium. Rittenhouse Square area is particularly nice.

Old City: Great if you want to be near the historic sites. Cobblestone streets, colonial buildings, some good restaurants and bars.

South Philly / Near Stadium: There are some hotels closer to the sports complex. Less exciting nightlife, but very convenient for match days.

University City: Near Penn's campus. Some hotel options, good food scene, a bit removed from Center City action.

Fishtown: Trendier area with great bars and restaurants. Fewer hotels but worth exploring.

Good news: Philadelphia is significantly more affordable than New York. Hotels, food, and drinks are all cheaper. Your budget stretches further here."

---

[TRANSPORT TO THE GAME]

"Getting to Lincoln Financial Field:

Subway (BSL): The Broad Street Line runs directly to NRG Station at the sports complex. This is the easiest way from Center City. It's cheap, fast, and drops you right there.

Regional Rail: SEPTA regional rail also has a Sports Complex station on game days.

Driving: The sports complex has parking, but traffic and parking fees add up. If you can, take transit.

Walking: If you're staying in South Philly, you might even be able to walk to the stadium.

Rideshare: Uber and Lyft work. Expect surge pricing after events. The sports complex is a defined area, so pickup logistics are manageable.

From the airport: PHL to Center City is only about 20-25 minutes on the Airport Line regional rail. Very convenient.

Pro tip: Take the Broad Street Line to the game. It's easy, cheap, and you avoid parking hassles. After the game, the trains will be packed, but they run frequently on event nights.

Tailgating is big in the parking lots before games. Eagles fans are legendary tailgaters, and that culture will carry over to World Cup matches.

That's Philadelphia! Full guide at worldcup26fanzone.com. History, cheesesteaks, and world-class soccer - what more could you want?"
