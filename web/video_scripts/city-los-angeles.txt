[MAP & KEY LOCATIONS]

"LA is massive, so let's break down the geography:

SoFi Stadium is in Inglewood, southwest of downtown LA. It's the home of the Rams and Chargers.

Key areas you should know:
- Hollywood: The iconic area - Walk of Fame, nightlife, touristy but fun
- Downtown LA (DTLA): Growing arts scene, great restaurants, urban vibe
- Santa Monica and Venice Beach: Coastal vibes, beach culture, the pier
- Beverly Hills: Luxury shopping, celebrity spotting
- Koreatown: Incredible food scene, 24-hour culture
- LAX Airport: Southwest of the city, actually pretty close to SoFi Stadium

The thing about LA - everything is spread out. You'll need to plan for traffic. But with matches at SoFi, you're already on the west side, so beaches and the coast are accessible."

---

[ONE THING TO KEEP IN MIND]

"Here's your LA survival tip:

TRAFFIC IS REAL. The stereotypes are true. Give yourself way more time than Google Maps says to get anywhere. Leaving 'right on time' means arriving late.

For match days, plan to arrive at SoFi early. Like, really early. The stadium area can get congested, and you don't want to miss kickoff because you're stuck on the 405.

On the positive side: LA weather in June and July is basically perfect. Mid-70s, sunny, low humidity. Just remember sunscreen - you WILL burn.

Also, LA is one of the most diverse food cities in the world. Thai Town, Koreatown, Little Tokyo, East LA for Mexican, Little Ethiopia - whatever cuisine you want, LA has world-class versions."

---

[LODGING SECTION]

"Lodging in LA:

Near SoFi / Inglewood: Some newer hotels have popped up near the stadium. Convenient for match days but less to do in the immediate area.

Santa Monica / West LA: Great option! Beach vibes, tons of restaurants, and you're on the same side of town as the stadium. The commute to SoFi isn't bad.

Downtown LA: Urban experience, arts district, rooftop bars. But you'll have a longer trek to SoFi through traffic.

Hollywood: Touristy, but iconic. Good nightlife. Moderate distance to the stadium.

Beverly Hills / West Hollywood: Upscale, great dining, but pricey.

Here's my tip: Stay on the WEST SIDE. Santa Monica, Culver City, Marina del Rey, even El Segundo. You'll be close to the stadium and the beach. Win-win.

LA is expensive. Budget accordingly. But there are also tons of hostels and budget options if you look."

---

[TRANSPORT TO THE GAME]

"Getting to SoFi Stadium:

Metro: The K Line now connects to the Inglewood station, a short shuttle or walk from SoFi. If you're staying anywhere near a Metro line, this is the move. Seriously - avoid driving if you can.

Driving: Parking exists but fills up fast and costs money. Tailgating culture isn't as big here as other cities.

Rideshare: Uber and Lyft work. Expect surge pricing. Set a pickup point AWAY from the main exits after the game.

From LAX: The stadium is only about 5 miles from the airport, so if you're flying in on match day, you're close. But match day traffic could still make it slow.

Pro tip: The new metro infrastructure near SoFi is a game-changer. Plan to use it. LA traffic on match days could be brutal, and the metro lets you skip all that stress.

That's Los Angeles! Full city guide at worldcup26fanzone.com. This could be where the World Cup winner is crowned!"
