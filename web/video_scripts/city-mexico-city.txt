[ONE THING TO KEEP IN MIND]

"Here's what you need to know about Mexico City:

ALTITUDE. Mexico City sits at 2,240 meters - about 7,350 feet above sea level. That's higher than Denver.

You will feel it, especially the first day or two. Some people get mild altitude sickness - headaches, shortness of breath when walking uphill, fatigue. Stay hydrated. Take it easy on day one.

The good news? By day two or three, most people adjust. Just be aware of it.

Other things: Mexico City's food scene is INCREDIBLE and cheap. Tacos, tlacoyos, tortas, mole - the flavors here are next level. Street food is generally safe in busy areas.

The city is safer than its reputation suggests, but use common sense. The tourist areas - Roma, Condesa, Polanco, Centro - are well-policed and full of visitors."
