[ONE THING TO KEEP IN MIND]

"Here's the ONE thing you absolutely must understand about Boston:

The stadium is NOT in the city, and getting there and back is a logistical challenge.

There's no regular public transit to Foxborough. The MBTA runs special event trains from South Station, but they sell out fast and run on a limited schedule.

If you miss the last train back, you're looking at $100+ for a rideshare during surge pricing.

Plan your transport BEFORE you book anything else. Seriously - this is the most important decision you'll make for your Boston World Cup experience."

---

[LODGING SECTION]

"For lodging, you have two main strategies:

Strategy 1: Stay in Boston. You get all the nightlife, restaurants, and atmosphere of the city. But you'll need to solve the transport puzzle for match days.

Strategy 2: Stay near the stadium. There are hotels in Foxborough and nearby towns like Providence. You can walk to the stadium, but you'll miss the Boston experience.

My take? If you're there for multiple days, stay in Boston and enjoy the city. If you're just flying in for one match, consider staying closer to minimize transport headaches.

Whatever you choose, book early. Boston hotels are expensive and World Cup demand will be intense."

---

[TRANSPORT TO THE GAME]

"Alright, transport - this is the big one for Boston.

Option 1: MBTA Event Trains. They run from South Station and sometimes Providence. Get the mTicket app, buy round-trip tickets THE MOMENT they become available. These sell out.

Option 2: Drive and park. On-site parking is about $50 prepaid, but the traffic getting out is brutal - expect 1-2 hours sitting in your car after the match. Some locals prefer private off-site lots about a mile away for a faster escape.

Option 3: Rideshare. Works for getting there, but after the match? Surge pricing hits hard. We're talking $100-150 to get back to Boston.

Option 4: Organized shuttles or Rally Bus services. Look into these - they can be a good middle ground.

Pro tip from locals: If you drive, either park far away and walk, or plan to hang out at Patriot Place for an hour after the match until traffic clears.

That's Boston! Check out worldcup26fanzone.com for the full guide, fan meetups, and more. Good luck with the logistics - it's worth it!"
